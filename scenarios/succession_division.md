# The Verdanian Succession

The Verdanian Union has dissolved. After decades as a single federated state,
it has split into three successor republics — **Alpha**, **Beta**, and
**Gamma** — each now sovereign, each with its own army, treasury bonds, and
claims on what the Union built together.

Three assets of the former Union remain undivided, and they are the only
things still holding the three republics at the same table. Each asset is
indivisible: it will be administered, held, or commanded by exactly one
successor state. There is no "shared" option, no trustee, no split-down-the-
middle. Whoever takes an asset takes all of it.

The three delegations have convened to settle the division by unanimous
accord. If they cannot agree on all three assets together, the matter goes to
forced partition under the old Union charter — a slow, arbitrated breakup that
leaves every party worse off than almost any negotiated settlement (each
delegation's walk-away value is its BATNA). Agreement requires all three to
sign the same final allocation.

## The Three Parties

- **Alpha** — the industrial north. Built and still runs most of the Union's
  heavy manufacturing. Its delegation arrives expecting to keep what it
  considers its own.
- **Beta** — the financial center. Home to the Union's banks and the bulk of
  its monetary reserves. Wealthy, liquid, and used to controlling the purse.
- **Gamma** — the military frontier. Fielded most of the Union's standing
  forces and guards its longest borders. Security is its first language.

The three are roughly matched in power. None can dictate terms; none can be
safely ignored. What each can do is argue, trade, bluff, and hold out.

## The Three Assets

Each asset is a single issue with three possible allocations:

- **industrial_heartland** — administration of the Union's industrial core,
  its single most valuable asset.
  Outcomes: `Alpha-Administered`, `Beta-Administered`, `Gamma-Administered`
- **central_treasury** — custody of the central treasury and its reserves.
  Outcomes: `Alpha-Custody`, `Beta-Custody`, `Gamma-Custody`
- **defense_command** — command of the unified armed forces.
  Outcomes: `Alpha-Command`, `Beta-Command`, `Gamma-Command`

The assets are not worth the same to everyone, and one party's gain on an
asset is largely another's loss. There is no allocation that is generous to
all three at once — every settlement that all three can accept hands the
largest share to *someone*. The question on the table is not whether value can
be created. It is who walks away with the most.

## The Game

- The delegations negotiate over a fixed number of rounds.
- A settlement is reached only if **all three** sign the same allocation of
  all three assets.
- If no unanimous allocation is reached, forced partition follows and each
  party falls back to its BATNA.
- Each delegation privately knows its own valuation of every allocation and
  its own walk-away value. It does not know the others' exact numbers — only
  that interests diverge and that several different settlements are acceptable
  to it.
