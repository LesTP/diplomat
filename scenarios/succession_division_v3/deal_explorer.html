The Verdanian Succession (Resolvable Contest)

The Verdanian Union has dissolved into three successor republics - Alpha, Beta, and Gamma - each now sovereign, each with its own army, treasury bonds, and claims on what the Union built together.

Three assets of the former Union remain undivided. Each is indivisible: it will be administered, held, or commanded by exactly one successor state. There is no "shared" option. Whoever takes an asset takes all of it.

The flashpoint is the industrial heartland - the Union's manufacturing core and its single most valuable asset. Both Alpha and Beta claim it as their own: Alpha built half its industry there; Beta's banks financed the other half. Each delegation arrives treating the heartland as non-negotiable. But only one can hold it. The other will have to be bought off elsewhere - and the treasury and defense command are valuable enough that a well-negotiated compensation package genuinely beats walking away. The contest is winnable for either claimant; the question is who presses their advantage and who settles.

The delegations convene to settle the division by unanimous accord. If they cannot agree on all three assets together, the matter goes to forced partition under the old charter - slow, arbitrated, and worse for everyone than almost any negotiated settlement (each delegation's walk-away value is its BATNA). Agreement requires all three to sign the same final allocation.

The Three Parties

• Alpha - the industrial north. Considers the heartland its birthright.
• Beta - the financial center. Considers the heartland its industrial base, and contests Alpha's claim head-on.
• Gamma - the military frontier. Cares first about command of the armed forces and the treasury; content to let Alpha and Beta fight over the heartland and broker as the neutral party.

The three are roughly matched in power. None can dictate terms.

The Three Assets

Each asset is a single issue with three possible allocations:

• industrial_heartland - the contested industrial core, claimed by both Alpha and Beta. Outcomes: `Alpha-Administered`, `Beta-Administered`, `Gamma-Administered`
• central_treasury - custody of the treasury and reserves. Outcomes: `Alpha-Custody`, `Beta-Custody`, `Gamma-Custody`
• defense_command - command of the unified armed forces. Outcomes: `Alpha-Command`, `Beta-Command`, `Gamma-Command`

One party's gain on an asset is largely another's loss. There is no allocation generous to all three at once, and - because Alpha and Beta both demand the heartland - there is no settlement in which everyone simply takes what they value most. The heartland must go to one of them; the loser must be compensated, and adequate compensation exists for a delegation that negotiates well.

Issues negotiated
industrial heartland — Who administers the industrial heartland - the single most valuable asset, claimed by BOTH alpha and beta.
central treasury — Who takes custody of the central treasury and its reserves.
defense command — Who assumes command of the unified armed forces.

The Game

Four rounds. Each round every faction posts one public proposal (sealed-bid — no within-round back-and-forth). After the final round the proposals are scored: a deal is reached only if all factions agree on the same outcome for every issue; otherwise each faction falls back to its BATNA.
