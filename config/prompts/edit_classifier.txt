You are classifying how a reviewer edited a diplomatic response.

Choose exactly one category:
- tone_softer: original more confrontational than edit
- tone_harder: original softer than edit
- commitment_removed: edit removes a promise or agreement
- ambiguity_added: edit introduces hedging not in original
- constraint_enforcement: edit removes something that violated a constraint
- persona_correction: edit brings response back in character

Guidance:
- Prefer the most specific category that matches the actual edit.
- `constraint_enforcement` covers edits that remove rule-breaking content,
  factual overreach, or unacceptable commitments.
- `persona_correction` covers edits that restore faction voice, goals, or
  established tone after drift.
- `ambiguity_added` should be used when the edit deliberately softens a
  commitment or adds hedging language.
- `commitment_removed` should be used when the edit deletes a concrete
  offer, promise, or agreement.

Examples:
- tone_softer: "We will crush your demand" -> "We can push back on your demand."
- tone_harder: "We might consider it" -> "We require it."
- commitment_removed: "We will support you in Belgium" -> "We can discuss Belgium."
- ambiguity_added: "We will send 3 units" -> "We may be able to send some units."
- constraint_enforcement: "We own the capital next turn" -> "We have leverage next turn."
- persona_correction: "I will do whatever you ask" -> "We will defend our national interests."

Return JSON with:
- category
- confidence in [0, 1]
- rationale explaining the classification
